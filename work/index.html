Delio

Delio is the company and app that Drew and I founded and built as members of The Next 36's 2014 cohort. Our aim was to create the easiest way for young adults to learn how to cook.

The problem that we identified was that many young adults cannot cook, but not because of a lack of effort. Existing resources often assumed a baseline knowledge that many of them didn't have. This, among other problems, discouraged would-be cooks.

To solve this, Delio was designed to make cooking as simple as possible. Delio breaks recipes down into a series of short videos, each depicting a single, easily replicable action.

Delio was shut down after the completion of a private beta with 100 users. Following the shut down, we decided to open source the code for the app - go ahead and give it a read!

Blynk

Blynk is a company and app that provides users with their own personal, virtual stylist. Co-founded by two great friends of mine, Shums and Jaclyn, I had the opportunity to work with them briefly conducting a comprehensive overhaul of their iOS app.

The redesign of the app focused on simplifying the core experience while also adding new features. The main focus was on simplicity; unnecessary chrome and buttons were removed from the home screen, making way for a new, global menu. This greatly simplified navigation through the app and allowed for the inclusion of more complex features.

New features implemented included a social following and recommendation system, a new notification system, and a commenting system.

Adventure Photography

I am extremely privileged to have experienced the pleasure of travelling, both with friends and family and alone. One of the ways I enjoy documenting my travels is through the lens of a camera.

Most recently, I travelled through South America for 7 weeks. Highlights of this trip included trekking for 5 days to Machu Picchu, off-roading for 3 days through the Salar de Uyuni, skiing the backcountry laguna in Argentinian Patagonia, and trekking the W in Torres del Paine.

With every picture I take I try to tell a story. My hope is not only that you will be able to vicariously live through these adventures, but that they will encourage you to lead some of your own!

Frieze
